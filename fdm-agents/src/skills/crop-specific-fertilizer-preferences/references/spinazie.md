# Spinach (spinazie) — nl_2773 (production), nl_2774 (seeds and propagation material)

Spinach is a short-cycle (35–55 days) leaf crop, almost always grown on contract for
processors. The combination of short cycle + leaf consumption makes **N management
dominant**: both uniform growth and leaf nitrate residue are contract requirements.

**Prefer:**
- **Early, generous base N before sowing** or just after sowing, followed by one
  top-dress mid-season for late-winter and summer sowings. For autumn / winter spinach a
  single application is often sufficient. Total N follows `advice.d_n_req`.
- Ammonium or mixed forms (KAS, ammoniumsulfaatsalpeter) on cold soils in spring —
  limits leaching before crop uptake.
- S per `advice.d_s_req` — spinach has a moderate S requirement.
- K as K₂SO₄ when chloride-free is a contract advantage; otherwise KCl is acceptable.
- B per `advice.d_b_req`.

**Avoid:**
- N close to harvest: leaf nitrate peaks and easily exceeds the contract limit and the
  EU limit (Regulation 1258/2011). Respect the N-free interval before harvest specified
  by the processor (typically 2–3 weeks; follow the contract).
- Fresh slurry close to sowing or emergence: uneven emergence, disease risk
  (*Pythium*), and unpredictable N mineralisation increase nitrate residue.
- KCl when the processor requires chloride-free product — check the contract.

**Extra attention:**
- For processing-industry spinach the three hard-limit parameters are nitrate, heavy
  metals, and pesticide residue. Fertilization mainly affects the first; flag this in
  the plan.
- On late-harvest (late summer / autumn) fields, late-applied N can accumulate as
  nitrate in cool weather — cap the rate deliberately, even if `advice.d_n_req` allows
  more.
- Cut-and-come-again spinach (multiple cuts) follows the same principle; an N
  application after each cut is acceptable, provided it is before the N-free interval
  of the final harvest.
