# Lettuce types and endive (sla en andijvie)

Includes:
- **Endive** (nl_2708 production, nl_2709 seed / propagation) — *Cichorium endivia*.
- **Iceberg lettuce** (nl_2767, nl_2768).
- **Radicchio rosso** (nl_2769, nl_2770).
- **Lettuce, other** (butterhead, batavia, lollo, oak-leaf, etc.) (nl_2771, nl_2772).

All are short-cycle (35–80 days) leaf crops in which **uniform N supply, Ca supply, and
residue management** are central. Almost always contract-grown.

**Prefer:**
- Split N with a generous base application before planting and one top-dress during
  active growth. For very short cycles (40–50 d) one application at planting plus
  fertigation top-up is often sufficient. Total according to `advice.d_n_req`.
- KAS / KAS+S or ammoniumsulfaatsalpeter as the main N source — provides stable
  nitrification without Cl load.
- K as K₂SO₄ when the contract requires chloride-free product; otherwise KCl is
  acceptable.
- Ca per `advice.d_ca_req` — Ca deficiency causes **tipburn / leaf-edge necrosis** in
  iceberg and butterhead lettuce, a classic quality defect. Foliar Ca sprays are a
  corrective rather than a substitute measure.
- B per `advice.d_b_req` — endive in particular is B-sensitive.

**Avoid:**
- N close to harvest — leaf nitrate easily exceeds EU limits (Regulation 1258/2011, with
  seasonal and regional differentiation for lettuce). Respect the N-free interval
  specified by the contract (typically ~2 weeks before harvest).
- Fresh slurry close to planting: *Sclerotinia*, *Botrytis*, and pathogen risk
  (including *E. coli*) for fresh-market product.
- Irregular irrigation combined with high N — worsens tipburn despite adequate
  `advice.d_ca_req` dosing.
- High N in early-spring crops under glass / plastic when destined for fresh market —
  high nitrate residue because of low light.

**Per type:**
- **Iceberg (nl_2767)**: most tipburn-prone; foliar Ca sprays in hot periods are
  standard practice alongside `advice.d_ca_req`.
- **Endive (nl_2708)**: moderate N requirement, moderately B-sensitive; the blanching
  procedure (tying / covering) is not a fertilizer issue.
- **Radicchio (nl_2769)**: slow grower; uniform N supply over a longer period; lower
  nitrate-residue pressure than head lettuce, but contract specifications can differ.
- **Lettuce, other (nl_2771)**: butterhead, batavia, oak-leaf, lollo — follow
  `advice.d_n_req` and the contract N-free interval; for multi-cut systems, lighter
  fertilization per cycle.

**Extra attention:**
- For protected cropping (plastic / tunnel) nitrate residue is higher because of lower
  light levels; apply `advice.d_n_req` cautiously and flag the residue risk in the plan.
- Glasshouse lettuce is out of scope for this skill — there, fertigation recipes lead.
