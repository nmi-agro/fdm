# Aardperen / topinambur (Jerusalem artichoke, *Helianthus tuberosus*) — nl_1949

Aardperen is **not** a beet. Botanically it is an Asteraceae (sunflower family),
perennial with tubers harvested as an annual crop. The crop is robust, scarcely
susceptible to most arable diseases, and has a low to moderate fertilizer requirement.

> **Catalogue note**: nl_2651 is the older "fodder beets (including Jerusalem artichoke)"
> code that lumps fodder beets and aardperen together. When the field is in fact
> aardperen, use **nl_1949** and the rules below. The sugar / fodder beet rules in
> `voederbieten.md` do **not** apply to aardperen.

**Prefer:**
- Low to moderate N: aardperen is N-efficient and prone to lodging at high N. Follow
  `advice.d_n_req` strictly; one application in spring, before ridging, is usually
  sufficient.
- K and P per `advice.d_k_req` / `advice.d_p_req` — tuber formation needs adequate K.
- KCl is acceptable — no chloride sensitivity.
- Well-matured organic matter applied in spring or the previous autumn. Aardperen
  responds well to organic matter, especially on light sandy soils.

**Avoid:**
- High N rates: weak stems (3 m+), lodging, and excessive haulm at the expense of tuber
  yield.
- Late N top-dress: wasted, because tuber bulking is largely complete by July.
- Fresh slurry close to planting — uneven emergence and salt stress on the seed tubers.

**Extra attention:**
- **Volunteer / weed problems in following crops**: aardperen is extremely persistent —
  tuber fragments left in the soil produce volunteer plants in subsequent crops for
  years. This is not a fertilizer issue but should be flagged in rotation advice, since
  many arable farmers underestimate this persistence.
- N release from crop residues: moderate (substantial haulm and tuber residue); factor
  this into next year's N planning.
- Aardperen is sometimes grown for energy / biogas; the fertilizer rules do not change,
  and `advice.d_n_req` accounts for the destination.
