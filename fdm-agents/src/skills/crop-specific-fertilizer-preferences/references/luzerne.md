# Luzerne / alfalfa (*Medicago sativa*) — nl_258

Luzerne is a deep-rooted, multi-year leguminous fodder crop. It is the strongest N₂-fixer
of the NL field crops and brings major rotation benefits (deep rooting, structural
improvement, residual N for the following crop).

**Prefer:**
- **Inoculation** with *Sinorhizobium meliloti* on first-time fields or fields without a
  recent luzerne history. Without the right rhizobia, fixation is poor and yield
  collapses.
- Adequate K, S and (modest) Ca/B inputs as a productive crop: follow `advice.d_k_req`,
  `advice.d_s_req`, `advice.d_b_req`, `advice.d_ca_req`. Luzerne is a heavy K and S user.
- Patentkali (K₂SO₄·MgSO₄) as an attractive K + Mg + S combination, especially on lighter
  soils.
- Liming when soil pH is low: luzerne is one of the most pH-sensitive arable crops in NL
  (rooting and Rhizobium activity drop sharply on acid soils). Treat low pH-KCl as a
  strong risk flag and consult `advice.d_ca_req`.
- Boron: luzerne is highly B-sensitive (growth disorders, poor regrowth). Consult
  `advice.d_b_req`.

**Avoid:**
- Mineral N: legumes fix their own N — additional N suppresses nodulation. Follow
  `advice.d_n_req`, which is normally near zero for established luzerne.
- Late-summer cuts immediately followed by heavy K applications — luzerne needs reserves
  going into winter; over-stimulation reduces winter hardiness.

**Extra attention:**
- N release: luzerne leaves substantial residual N (and structural benefit) for the
  following crop — note this in rotation-level N planning.
- Cl tolerance: luzerne is moderately Cl-tolerant; KCl is acceptable when soil K is the
  limiting factor and Mg is otherwise covered, but K₂SO₄ / patentkali is preferred for
  combined K + S supply.
